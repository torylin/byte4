Is the data complete?

Several aspects of the data are incomplete. For example, my mobile device would not always succeed in predicting activity, leading to a very large number of "Null" activity predictions that I excluded from the visualization. Furthermore, some activity predictions fell under multiple categories (["stationary," "automotive"]). As the meaning of a multi-category prediction is unclear, I also excluded these predictions from the visualization. The following plot displays the number of records per activity prior to the removal of the aforementioned predictions.

In addition, the sampling of the data, while not necessarily incomplete, is not constant over time—it appears in all three cases that samples are taken whenever there is significant movement or activity rather than, for example, every 30 minutes. This somewhat skews the results of frequency plots because there are fewer records when my phone does not move at all (for example, when I am asleep in bed) than when it is moving (for example, when I am at school), making it seem as though I spend more time at places where my phone moves (e.g. school) than at places where it does not (e.g. at home in bed). Graphs of the number of records at each hour of the day confirm that more records are taken during active hours than during sleeping hours.

Is the data coherent?

Given that the sampling of the data is not constant over time, the distribution of the data makes sense and matches my expectations (somewhat sadly). As a student, I would expect that the vast majority of my time is spent either at school or at home, which is where I'm most often sampled. The few locations recorded outside of those limited settings occurred on the weekends, when I would be free to travel. My activity data also seems to make sense—most of the records indicate that I am stationary, which is consistent with the fact that I am usually in class or working on classwork. My second most frequent activity is recorded as walking (consistent with traveling to and from public transportation, around campus, on weekends, etc.), and automotive transport is the third (consistent with taking the bus to and from school, or driving somewhere on the weekends).

Is the data correct?

As this data originates from my own behavior, I am at least able to verify the accuracy of the location map almost completely. I can see that on Mondays and Wednesdays I travel from my home to campus around 12 PM (when I have class), while on Tuesdays and Thursdays I make the trip between 10 AM and 11 AM (I have class at 10:30 AM). The map further indicates that I usually return home in the evenings, which I know to be true. My trips to the Waterfront and Ross Park Mall on October 13 were accurately recorded in the data, as was my late-night trip to Whole Foods the day before.

On the other hand, the activity data contains some inaccuracies, as at no point in the past two weeks did I spend any time cycling. It's unclear to me why my mobile device predicted it as the most likely activity for a given record (perhaps I was on a slow-moving bus?). However, outside of those few inexplicable records, the activity data is correct as far as I can tell—it indicates that I am most often stationary, then next most often walking, which I know to be true due to class and commuting, respectively.

Likewise, the address data is not completely correct, probably due to lack of precision in the phone's ability to gauge locatiion. For example, the raw data records often placed me at a number of different nearby addresses on my residential street rather than at my own apartment only, which I had to correct in the data-cleaning process by aggregating those records. Likewise, the initial data recorded various locations on the CMU campus as different addresses (perhaps technically true, but not useful for my analysis), which I again aggregated in the data cleaning process.
